Deep dive 01

Adjusted pricing & business model

Decide
Context

Our model monetizes the participant with the weakest network effect and keeps the strongest one free: standard buyers pay an activation fee plus a success fee on purchase; Swappers pay neither, because their future sales mandate is the center of our flywheel. 

On top of that, the initial agent pricing created friction exactly where we need cooperation. They felt like the follow-up mandate was not guaranteed but they had to share their commission on properties that they were sure will sell.

What we learned
  • Friction on the first transaction kills agent adoption
  • We cannot lose any swappers because of activation fees
  • Buyers are willing to pay much more than we asked for initially

Deep dive 03

Strategy to get more off-market supply

Discuss
Context

Off-market supply is the product — our promise to buyers is timing, not inventory. The thesis: agents should share opportunities early because it is economically rational (it increases their odds of winning the seller's next mandate through us), not because a contract forces them. Four true off-market properties says the incentive isn't yet felt, and our process makes sharing costly: several follow-ups per listing, constrained by our own capacity.

What we learned
  • Demand pulls focus from supply: the more leads we get, the less time goes into agents.
  • Publication is a multi-touch process today — it needs owners, tooling and cadence.
  • 14 partners are onboarded; activation, not signing, is the bottleneck.
Options To complete
A — Process: dedicated supply owner + weekly agent cadence + publication SLA …
B — Automation: AI-assisted follow-ups and one-click listing intake (ties into the Automation & AI roadmap) …
C — Incentive: sharpen the mandate-back promise per shared property (make early sharing visibly pay) …

Forward

Buy Back Guarantee — the missing piece of the Swapper journey

Discuss

A notarised commitment to buy the Swapper's current home at a floor price if it hasn't sold in 6 months — certainty that makes their bank finance the next home. Framed as a forward purchase commitment, not insurance, so it stays outside FINMA supervision.

The gap it closes

✕
Equity is locked
The down payment sits in the current home.
✕
Two mortgages fail affordability
Banks won't carry both homes.
✕
Sale price & timing uncertain
No one guarantees the old home sells.
✕
Conditional offers get rejected
Sellers pick credible buyers in a tight market.

US platforms (HomeLight, Knock, Orchard) prove the model. Nothing exists at scale in Switzerland.

The structure — two sister vehicles, fully ring-fenced

One notarised transfer (customer → CasaBuy) — no double notary costs, no double gains-tax event. The bank's comfort letter points at CasaBuy's committed capital.

Why an SPV — six options scored

Casarico issues15
Refolio issues16
Joint venture21
Insurance partner19
Bank guarantee15
Independent SPV31

Scored across simplicity, capital efficiency, liability isolation, investor friendliness, regulatory burden, scalability and bank confidence. The SPV wins everything that matters at scale; its only cost is a heavier setup quarter.

At an 85% floor, a rational seller cuts the asking price long before surrendering 15% — expected activation only ~10%.

Reference

Strategy on a page

Inform

Buyers don't lack listings — they lack timing. Casarico compresses the time between an agent's decision to sell and the buyer knowing about it. We compete on information velocity and trusted relationships; search is the entry point, owning the transaction workflow is the goal.

The flywheel

One promise per persona

Buyer
Find the next home before it's publicly listed.
Swapper
Secure the next home before selling the current one.
Agent
Win additional seller mandates through qualified Swappers.

How the pieces connect

  • Model — buyers pay activation + success fee; Swappers are free because their mandate is worth more.
  • Supply — early sharing must be economically rational for agents, never contractually forced.
  • Buy-back — conditional purchase certainty turns timing risk into a financial product.
  • Expansion — Geneva proves, Zurich validates portability, national only once the engine repeats.
  • North star — the operating system for residential moves: discovery, financing certainty, purchase, sale and orchestration in one workflow.

Risks & mitigations

Insufficient proprietary supplyAgent incentive alignment (deep dive 03)
Weak agent engagementMandate-back flywheel, partner activation
Matching quality at scaleAutomation roadmap
Interest-rate environmentConditional structure of buy-back, disciplined pilot
Capital needs of buy-back guaranteesSPV structure, external capital partners

Decision rule: every choice should strengthen the flywheel of Swappers, proprietary supply, trusted execution and transaction ownership. Incremental revenue that dilutes this positioning is rejected.
